This question has three parts.

1) Think about how a total solar eclipse is only visible from a fairly narrow strip across the Earth. Draw a diagram (and upload / paste it into your answer below) and describe how naked eye astronomers could have used this information to deduce the distance to the moon. 

2) Explain why they would need to know i) the distance between any two observers and ii) be able to agree on the time (ie they had "synchronised") clocks. 

3) Explain why once you know the distance to the Moon you would also know its size.

Your answer:
IMG2.PNG
1.
Pa = parallax angle at the moon

d = distance from the moon to the earth

oA and oB = observer A and observer B

do = distance between observers.

 

The observers in different spots on the Earth are able to view the solar eclipse from 2 distinct spots on Earth (oA and oB)

By knowing both Pa and the distance between observers (do), they are able to deduce a triangle. Then, using trigonometry, they can calculate d.

2.
The distance between observers is critical in trigonometry because knowing the angle and length between them allows you to extract the rest of the triangle. 
Its also important to measure the moon at the exact same time because otherwise you risk measuring it at 2 different points in the night sky. Which would give inaccurate results. 
3.
Once the size is deduced, you can figure out how big it is in your field of vision, which provides an angular size. This, combined with a distance, allows us to obtain the true size of the moon.

a) Explain why the colours of stars tell us their temperatures, but the colours of Post-It notes do not tell us the temperatures of the Post-It notes. [1 point]

b) Could I ever hope to detect a star with the same colours as most of these Post-It notes? [1 point]

 

Your answer:
A)
Stars are huge and emit light due to their immense heat. The color of a star can tell us about its temperature; for example, a blue star is hotter than a red star because it emits higher-frequency light with a shorter wavelength.

The reason Post-it notes are not the same is that they do not emit their own light. Any color Post-it note looks the color it does because it reflects that color and absorbs the rest. For example, a blue Post-it note is blue because the Post-it note absorbs every color expect Blue, which it reflects.

B)
All stars, depending on their heat, would be somewhere on the light spectrum as they get hotter.
Red, Orange, Yellow, White, Blue.
Colors like green or pink aren't possible because they aren't on the blackbody radiation graph.
